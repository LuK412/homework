Welcome and thank you for participating in the experiment.

Please read the following instructions carefully and raise your hand if you have any questions.

In this experiment, participants are randomized into groups of three.
Within each group, participants are assigned one of three colors (red, blue, green).
This means that each group consists of one red player, one blue player and one green player.

All players are endowed with {{ Constants.endowment }}.

The red player of each group will choose one of 6 possible payoff alterations which affect the payoffs of all participants in his/her group. The table summarizing the payoff alterations is displayed below. All players in this experiment see the same table.
Blue and green players are asked to indicate which allocation they would like red to choose. Their decision, however, does not affect their payoffs and only the decision of red players determine the payoffs of the members of each group.
There is only one round in the experiment. Therefore, every participant makes only one decision.

We imagine that it may be difficult for red to decide what s/he ought to do in this situation.
We therefore asked other students to formulate an advice. This advice will be displayed to all players on the page which asks for their decisions. The advice is the same for all players (i.e. all groups) in this experiment.
